Privacy Policy

Last Updated: December 2025

Year In Health (“the App,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) is developed by Gal Aviel. We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring you have complete control over your health data. This Privacy Policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of information when you use our application.

By using Year In Health, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy. Our fundamental principle is simple: Your data belongs to you.

IMPORTANT: NOT MEDICAL ADVICE

This App is intended for informational, educational, and entertainment purposes only. It is not a medical device and is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease or health condition. The data presented, including but not limited to heart rate zones, activity trends, sleep analysis, and other health metrics, are estimates derived from Apple Health data and should not be considered clinically accurate measurements.

Always seek the advice of your physician or other qualified health provider with any questions you may have regarding a medical condition or before starting any new fitness regime. Never disregard professional medical advice or delay in seeking it because of something you have read in this App. If you think you may have a medical emergency, call your doctor or emergency services immediately.

1. Data Minimization & Local Processing

Year In Health operates with a strict "zero-knowledge" architecture regarding your personal health information. This means:

  • No Backend Servers: We do not operate a backend server to store user health data. We have no database of user records.
  • Local Processing: All data retrieval, aggregation, analysis, calculation, chart generation, and summary creation occur 100% locally on your iPhone using the device's own processor.
  • No Data Transfer: Your health data is never transmitted, uploaded, synced, or shared with us or any third-party servers by the App. It remains strictly on your device (and in your personal iCloud backups, if enabled by you).

2. Integration with Apple HealthKit

The App’s core functionality relies on reading data from Apple's HealthKit framework. We request read-only access to specific data types solely to visualize your year in health. We do not write data back to HealthKit.

We only access data types that you explicitly authorize via the iOS system permission prompt. Depending on the features you use, requested data types may include:

  • Activity: Step count, walking/running distance, and active energy burned (calories).
  • Sleep: Sleep analysis data to generate sleep duration and consistency insights.
  • Heart: Resting heart rate, walking average heart rate, and heart rate variability (if applicable) for trend analysis.
  • Workouts: Workout types, duration, and associated routes to summarize your fitness activities.

You retain full control over these permissions. You can review or revoke access at any time by navigating to:
Settings app → Health → Data Access & Devices → Year In Health.

3. Analytics & Tracking

We prioritize your privacy over data collection.

  • No Third-Party Trackers: The App does not contain SDKs from advertising networks, social media platforms, or third-party analytics providers (such as Google Analytics, Mixpanel, or Facebook Pixel). We do not track your individual behavior, tap history, or precise location.
  • Apple App Analytics: If you have opted-in to share "App Analytics" with developers within your iOS Privacy Settings, Apple provides us with anonymized, aggregated data. This may include installation counts, crash reports, and retention rates. This data is completely anonymous and cannot be used to identify you or access your health information.

4. User Profile & Preferences

Any personal information you enter into the App, such as your name for the recap summary or your target year preference, is stored locally on your device using secure iOS storage mechanisms (NSUserDefaults/AppStorage). This information is used solely for customizing the user interface and generating personalized summaries. It is never transmitted to us.
